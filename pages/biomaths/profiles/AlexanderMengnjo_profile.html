Contribution to Research and Development of Science

Models of Nerve Pulse generation with account of both electrical and mechanical activities of the Nerve cell.

Models of the nerve Impulse have been formulated by Hodgkin and Huxley in the late 50â€™s and further elaborated by Fitzhug and Nagumo. In these models, they viewed the nerve pulse as a purely electrical process. However, emerging experimental evidences revealed that some physical aspects of the nerve could also be responsible for this process. Heimburg and Jackson in 2007 formulated a model for the nerve pulse based exclusively on the physical processes of the nerve cell. We introduce a model that takes into account both the electrical and the Physical processes of the nerve cell. Using this model, we have generated, in the steady state, some coherent profiles of possible nerve impulses.
